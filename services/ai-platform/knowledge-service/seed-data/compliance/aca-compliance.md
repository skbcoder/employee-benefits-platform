# Affordable Care Act (ACA) Compliance

## Overview

The Affordable Care Act (ACA), also known as the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, imposes requirements on applicable large employers (ALEs) regarding health coverage offerings. This document outlines the company's obligations and compliance procedures.

## Applicable Large Employer (ALE) Status

An employer is considered an ALE if it employed an average of 50 or more full-time employees (including full-time equivalent employees) during the prior calendar year. Full-time is defined as 30 or more hours per week, or 130 hours per month.

The company qualifies as an ALE and must comply with the ACA's employer shared responsibility provisions (Section 4980H) and reporting requirements (Sections 6055 and 6056).

## Employer Shared Responsibility (Employer Mandate)

### Section 4980H(a) — Offering Coverage
The company must offer minimum essential coverage (MEC) to at least 95% of its full-time employees and their dependents. Failure to do so may result in a penalty of $2,970 per full-time employee (indexed annually) minus the first 30 employees if any full-time employee receives a premium tax credit through the marketplace.

### Section 4980H(b) — Affordability and Minimum Value
The coverage offered must meet affordability and minimum value standards:

**Affordability**: The employee's required contribution for self-only coverage must not exceed 9.12% of household income (indexed annually). Safe harbors available:
- W-2 Safe Harbor: Employee's share does not exceed 9.12% of their W-2 wages
- Rate of Pay Safe Harbor: Employee's share does not exceed 9.12% of their hourly rate x 130 hours per month
- Federal Poverty Line Safe Harbor: Employee's share does not exceed 9.12% of the federal poverty line for a single individual

**Minimum Value**: The plan must cover at least 60% of the total allowed costs of benefits. All company medical plans meet the minimum value standard.

## Essential Health Benefits

ACA requires non-grandfathered group health plans in the small group market to cover 10 categories of essential health benefits. While large group plans are not required to cover all 10 categories, our plans voluntarily cover:

1. Ambulatory patient services (outpatient care)
2. Emergency services
3. Hospitalization
4. Maternity and newborn care
5. Mental health and substance use disorder services
6. Prescription drugs
7. Rehabilitative and habilitative services and devices
8. Laboratory services
9. Preventive and wellness services and chronic disease management
10. Pediatric services, including oral and vision care

## Preventive Care Mandate

ACA requires all non-grandfathered health plans to cover certain preventive services at no cost-sharing when delivered by in-network providers:

- Evidence-based items rated A or B by the U.S. Preventive Services Task Force
- Immunizations recommended by the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices
- Preventive care and screenings for infants, children, and adolescents supported by HRSA
- Preventive care and screenings for women supported by HRSA

Examples include:
- Annual wellness visits
- Blood pressure and cholesterol screening
- Diabetes screening for adults with high blood pressure
- Colorectal cancer screening for adults 45+
- Immunizations (flu, hepatitis, HPV, etc.)
- Depression screening
- Contraceptive methods and counseling

## Reporting Requirements

### Form 1095-C (Employer-Provided Health Insurance Offer and Coverage)
The company must furnish Form 1095-C to each full-time employee by January 31 of the following year, documenting:
- Months in which MEC was offered
- Employee's share of the lowest-cost self-only monthly premium
- Safe harbor code used for affordability

### Form 1094-C (Transmittal of Employer-Provided Health Insurance)
The company must file Form 1094-C (transmittal form) along with all Forms 1095-C with the IRS by February 28 (paper) or March 31 (electronic) of the following year.

## Measurement, Administrative, and Stability Periods

For variable hour and seasonal employees, the company uses the look-back measurement method:

- **Standard Measurement Period**: October 15 through October 14 (12 months)
- **Administrative Period**: October 15 through December 31 (2.5 months)
- **Standard Stability Period**: January 1 through December 31 (12 months)

Employees who average 30+ hours per week during the measurement period are treated as full-time during the stability period and must be offered coverage.

## Nondiscrimination Requirements

The company's self-insured health plans must comply with Section 105(h) nondiscrimination requirements. Plans may not discriminate in favor of highly compensated individuals with respect to eligibility or benefits. Annual nondiscrimination testing is performed to ensure compliance.

## Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC)

ACA requires the company to provide a Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC) to participants and beneficiaries:
- At open enrollment
- Upon initial enrollment
- Within 7 business days of request
- At least 60 days before any material plan modification

SBCs must follow the standard format prescribed by HHS and include coverage examples, cost-sharing details, and coverage limitations.

## Mental Health Parity

The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA), reinforced by ACA, requires that mental health and substance use disorder benefits are no more restrictive than medical/surgical benefits in terms of financial requirements and treatment limitations. Our plans comply by applying the same deductibles, copays, and coinsurance to mental health and medical benefits.

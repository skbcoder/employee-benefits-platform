# Claims and Coverage FAQ

## Filing Claims

### Q: How do I file a medical claim?
A: Most claims are filed automatically by your healthcare provider. If you need to file a claim manually (e.g., for out-of-network services), submit a claim form with itemized receipts to the plan administrator. Claims must be filed within 12 months of the date of service.

### Q: How long does it take to process a claim?
A: In-network claims are typically processed within 15-30 days. Out-of-network claims may take up to 45 days. Urgent care claims are processed within 72 hours. You can check claim status through the insurance carrier's member portal.

### Q: What is an Explanation of Benefits (EOB)?
A: An EOB is a statement from the insurance carrier that explains what was billed, what the plan paid, and what you owe. An EOB is not a bill — you will receive a separate bill from the provider for any amount you owe. Always compare your EOB with provider bills before paying.

### Q: What does "not a covered benefit" mean on my EOB?
A: This means the service is excluded from your plan's coverage. Common exclusions include cosmetic procedures, experimental treatments, and services not deemed medically necessary. Review your Summary Plan Description for a complete list of exclusions and limitations.

## Prescription Drug Coverage

### Q: How do I know if my medication is covered?
A: Check the plan's prescription drug formulary, available through the insurance carrier's website or by calling the pharmacy benefits manager. Medications are classified into four tiers with different copay levels (generic, preferred brand, non-preferred brand, specialty).

### Q: My medication requires prior authorization. What do I do?
A: Your prescribing doctor must submit a prior authorization request to the plan. The plan will review and respond within 72 hours for urgent requests or 15 days for standard requests. If denied, your doctor can submit additional clinical documentation or request an exception.

### Q: Can I use mail-order pharmacy for maintenance medications?
A: Yes. All plans offer a mail-order pharmacy option for maintenance medications (medications taken regularly for chronic conditions). Mail-order prescriptions are filled for a 90-day supply at 2.5x the 30-day copay, providing a cost savings.

### Q: What if I need a brand-name drug when a generic is available?
A: If a generic equivalent is available, you will pay the generic copay plus the difference in cost between the generic and brand-name drug. If your doctor certifies that the brand-name drug is medically necessary, the plan may waive the cost difference with prior authorization.

## Dental and Vision Claims

### Q: Do I need pre-authorization for dental work?
A: Pre-authorization is recommended but not required for procedures expected to exceed $300. Pre-authorization helps you understand your expected costs before treatment. Contact the dental plan administrator to request pre-authorization.

### Q: Can I use my vision benefit for LASIK surgery?
A: The Premium vision plan offers a 15% discount or $100 off per eye for LASIK/PRK at participating providers. The Basic vision plan does not include LASIK discounts. LASIK is not covered as a plan benefit under either plan, but the discount can significantly reduce the cost.

### Q: How often can I get new glasses?
A: Under the Basic vision plan, frames are covered every 24 months and lenses every 12 months. Under the Premium vision plan, both frames and lenses are covered every 12 months. You can get new glasses more frequently, but the plan benefit will only apply per the schedule above.

## Coverage Situations

### Q: Am I covered when traveling out of state?
A: Yes. Emergency care is covered anywhere in the United States at in-network rates. For non-emergency care, coverage depends on your plan's network. The Platinum plan also includes international emergency coverage. Always seek in-network providers when possible.

### Q: What if I need to see a specialist?
A: Most plans do not require a referral to see an in-network specialist. Simply schedule an appointment with the specialist and provide your insurance information. Out-of-network specialists will result in higher out-of-pocket costs.

### Q: Is mental health treatment covered?
A: Yes. All plans cover mental health and substance abuse treatment at parity with medical/surgical benefits. This means the same deductibles, copays, and coinsurance apply. Outpatient therapy copays range from $10 to $30 depending on your plan. Inpatient mental health treatment is covered at the same rate as hospital inpatient care. Telehealth mental health visits are also covered.

### Q: Are preventive services really free?
A: Yes. Under the ACA, all plans must cover preventive services at no cost when you use an in-network provider. This includes annual checkups, cancer screenings, immunizations, and other preventive care. Diagnostic services (when you have symptoms or a condition) are subject to normal cost-sharing.

## Coordination of Benefits

### Q: I have two insurance plans. How does coordination of benefits work?
A: When you have dual coverage, the primary plan pays first, and the secondary plan may cover remaining costs up to 100% of allowed charges. Generally, the plan covering you as an employee is primary, and the plan covering you as a dependent is secondary. For dependent children, the "birthday rule" applies — the parent whose birthday falls earlier in the year has the primary plan.

### Q: My spouse and I both work here. Can we both have employee coverage?
A: Yes. Each of you can enroll as an employee under your own coverage. However, neither can be covered as both an employee and a dependent. You may choose to each have employee-only coverage or have one person cover the other as a dependent — compare the total cost of each option.

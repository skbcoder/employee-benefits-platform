# Benefits Enrollment Frequently Asked Questions

## Getting Started

### Q: When can I enroll in benefits?
A: You can enroll during three windows: (1) As a new hire within 30 days of your eligibility date, (2) During annual open enrollment (November 1-30), or (3) Within 30 days of a qualifying life event such as marriage, birth of a child, or loss of other coverage.

### Q: What happens if I miss the enrollment deadline?
A: If you miss the new hire enrollment window, you will be automatically enrolled in the Basic medical plan only. All other benefits will be waived until the next open enrollment period. If you miss the open enrollment deadline, your current elections carry forward unchanged.

### Q: How long does it take for my enrollment to be processed?
A: Most enrollments are processed within 24-48 hours. You can check the status of your enrollment at any time using the enrollment status tool. The lifecycle is: SUBMITTED (received) → PROCESSING (being reviewed) → COMPLETED (active). If your enrollment shows DISPATCH_FAILED, it will be automatically retried.

### Q: Can I make changes to my enrollment after submitting?
A: You cannot change your enrollment after submission unless you experience a qualifying life event. During open enrollment, you can submit a new enrollment that replaces your current elections before the deadline. Contact HR Benefits if you believe there is an error in your enrollment.

## Choosing a Plan

### Q: Which medical plan should I choose?
A: It depends on your expected healthcare usage. The Basic plan has the lowest premium but the highest out-of-pocket costs — best if you rarely see a doctor. The Silver plan balances cost and coverage. The Gold plan is good for families or frequent healthcare users. The Platinum plan provides maximum coverage for those who want comprehensive care with the lowest copays.

### Q: What's the difference between a deductible and out-of-pocket maximum?
A: The deductible is the amount you pay before the plan starts covering costs (except for preventive care, which is always free). The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you will pay in a plan year — after reaching this amount, the plan covers 100% of covered services.

### Q: Are my current doctors in-network?
A: Each plan has a different network size. The Basic plan has 5,000+ providers, Silver has 8,000+, Gold has 12,000+, and Platinum has 15,000+. You can search for your doctor at the plan's provider directory website. Contact HR Benefits for the specific network directory links.

### Q: Can I have medical, dental, vision, and life insurance all at the same time?
A: Yes. You can enroll in any combination of the available benefit types. Each benefit type is independent — you can choose a Gold medical plan with a Basic dental plan and Premium vision plan, for example.

### Q: What is an HSA and am I eligible?
A: A Health Savings Account (HSA) is a tax-advantaged savings account for medical expenses. You are eligible if you enroll in the Basic or Silver medical plan (both are HSA-eligible high-deductible health plans). The company contributes $500 (individual) or $1,000 (family) annually to your HSA if you choose the Basic plan. HSA funds roll over year to year and are yours to keep.

## Costs and Premiums

### Q: How much does the employer pay toward my benefits?
A: The employer contributes 75% of the employee-only premium for the Silver plan ($168.75/month). This contribution applies to whichever plan you select. For dependent coverage, the employer contributes 50% of the additional dependent cost on the Silver plan.

### Q: Are my premiums deducted pre-tax or post-tax?
A: Medical, dental, and vision premiums are deducted pre-tax through the Section 125 cafeteria plan, reducing your taxable income. Supplemental life insurance premiums are post-tax.

### Q: When do premium deductions start?
A: For new hires, deductions begin with the first payroll period after your benefits effective date. For open enrollment changes, new deductions begin in January.

## Dependents

### Q: Who qualifies as a dependent?
A: Eligible dependents include your legal spouse or registered domestic partner, and your children (biological, adopted, or step) up to age 26. Disabled dependents who became disabled before age 26 may continue regardless of age with proof of disability.

### Q: What documents do I need to add a dependent?
A: You need proof of relationship: marriage certificate for a spouse, birth certificate or adoption decree for children, domestic partnership registration for a domestic partner.

### Q: My spouse has insurance through their employer. Should I add them to my plan?
A: Compare the costs and coverage of both plans. If your spouse's plan provides good coverage at a reasonable cost, it may be more cost-effective to keep separate coverage. You can also have dual coverage — the primary plan pays first, and the secondary plan may cover remaining costs.

## Life Events and Changes

### Q: I just got married. What do I need to do?
A: Submit a qualifying life event change within 30 days of your marriage date. You can add your spouse to your medical, dental, vision, and life insurance. You'll need to provide a copy of your marriage certificate.

### Q: I'm having a baby. When should I enroll?
A: You have 30 days from the birth or adoption date to add your child to your benefits. Coverage for newborns is retroactive to the date of birth if enrolled within 30 days. Submit your enrollment change as soon as possible after the birth and provide a birth certificate.

### Q: I'm leaving the company. What happens to my benefits?
A: Your benefits continue through the last day of the month in which you terminate. You are eligible for COBRA continuation coverage, which allows you to keep your current plan for up to 18 months by paying the full premium plus a 2% admin fee. You'll receive a COBRA election notice within 14 days of your qualifying event.

## Claims and Issues

### Q: My enrollment is stuck in PROCESSING status. What should I do?
A: Enrollments typically process within 24-48 hours. If your enrollment has been in PROCESSING status for more than 48 hours, contact HR Benefits. A DISPATCH_FAILED status means the system is automatically retrying — this usually resolves on its own.

### Q: I received a claim denial. How do I appeal?
A: You have 180 days to file an appeal. Submit your appeal in writing to the plan administrator, including any supporting documentation. Appeals are reviewed by someone other than the person who made the initial denial. You will receive a decision within 30-60 days depending on the type of claim.

### Q: Where can I find my plan documents?
A: Summary Plan Descriptions (SPDs), Summary of Benefits and Coverage (SBC), and plan certificates are available through the HR Benefits portal. Contact HR Benefits if you need a physical copy.

# Dental Plan Options

## Overview

The company offers two dental plan options. Both plans cover preventive care at 100% with in-network providers and provide coverage for basic and major dental services.

## Plan Comparison

### Basic Dental Plan

The Basic dental plan covers essential dental care including preventive services, basic restorative work, and emergency dental treatment.

- Monthly Premium: $25 (employee only), $55 (employee + spouse), $50 (employee + children), $80 (family)
- Annual Deductible: $75 individual / $225 family
- Annual Maximum Benefit: $1,500 per person
- Preventive Services (cleanings, exams, x-rays): 100% covered, no deductible
- Basic Services (fillings, simple extractions): 80% covered after deductible
- Major Services (crowns, bridges, dentures): 50% covered after deductible
- Orthodontia: Not covered
- Waiting Period: No waiting period for preventive; 6 months for basic; 12 months for major
- Network: Delta Dental PPO network

### Premium Dental Plan

The Premium dental plan provides enhanced coverage including orthodontia, implants, and higher annual maximums.

- Monthly Premium: $55 (employee only), $120 (employee + spouse), $105 (employee + children), $170 (family)
- Annual Deductible: $50 individual / $150 family
- Annual Maximum Benefit: $3,000 per person
- Preventive Services (cleanings, exams, x-rays): 100% covered, no deductible
- Basic Services (fillings, simple extractions, root canals): 90% covered after deductible
- Major Services (crowns, bridges, dentures, implants): 70% covered after deductible
- Orthodontia: 50% covered, $2,500 lifetime maximum per person (children and adults)
- Implants: Covered under major services at 70%
- Waiting Period: No waiting period for any service
- Network: Delta Dental PPO and Premier network (broader access)
- Additional Benefits: Two additional cleanings per year for periodontal patients

## Preventive Services Details

Both plans cover the following preventive services at 100% with no deductible:
- Routine oral exams: 2 per year
- Cleanings (prophylaxis): 2 per year
- Bitewing x-rays: 1 set per year
- Full mouth/panoramic x-rays: 1 every 3 years
- Fluoride treatment: 1 per year (for children under 16)
- Sealants: 1 per tooth, per lifetime (for children under 14)

## Out-of-Network Coverage

Both plans provide out-of-network coverage at reduced benefit levels. Out-of-network providers may balance bill the difference between their charges and the plan's allowed amount. Employees are encouraged to use in-network providers to minimize out-of-pocket costs.

## Coordination of Benefits

If an employee or dependent is covered by more than one dental plan, the plans will coordinate benefits so that total payments do not exceed 100% of the allowed dental charges. The plan that covers the individual as the employee (rather than as a dependent) is considered primary.

## Pre-authorization

Pre-authorization is recommended for any dental procedure expected to exceed $300. While not required, pre-authorization helps the employee understand their coverage and expected out-of-pocket costs before treatment begins.

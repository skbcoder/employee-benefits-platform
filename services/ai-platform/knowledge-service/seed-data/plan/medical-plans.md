# Medical Plan Options

## Overview

The company offers four medical plan tiers through our preferred provider network. All plans include preventive care at no cost when using in-network providers, prescription drug coverage, mental health and substance abuse coverage, and telehealth services.

## Plan Comparison

### Basic Plan

The Basic plan is the most affordable option with lower premiums but higher out-of-pocket costs. Best suited for healthy individuals who rarely visit the doctor.

- Monthly Premium: $125 (employee only), $375 (employee + spouse), $300 (employee + children), $500 (family)
- Annual Deductible: $3,000 individual / $6,000 family
- Out-of-Pocket Maximum: $8,000 individual / $16,000 family
- Primary Care Visit: $40 copay after deductible
- Specialist Visit: $75 copay after deductible
- Emergency Room: $350 copay after deductible
- Prescription Drugs: $15 generic / $40 preferred brand / $75 non-preferred / 30% specialty
- Preventive Care: 100% covered (no cost)
- Telehealth: $20 copay
- Hospital Inpatient: 30% coinsurance after deductible
- Outpatient Surgery: 30% coinsurance after deductible
- Network: Standard provider network (5,000+ providers in-network)

### Silver Plan

- Monthly Premium: $225 (employee only), $575 (employee + spouse), $475 (employee + children), $750 (family)
- Annual Deductible: $1,500 individual / $3,000 family
- Out-of-Pocket Maximum: $5,500 individual / $11,000 family
- Primary Care Visit: $30 copay (no deductible required)
- Specialist Visit: $55 copay after deductible
- Emergency Room: $250 copay after deductible
- Prescription Drugs: $10 generic / $35 preferred brand / $60 non-preferred / 25% specialty
- Preventive Care: 100% covered (no cost)
- Telehealth: $15 copay
- Hospital Inpatient: 20% coinsurance after deductible
- Outpatient Surgery: 20% coinsurance after deductible
- Mental Health: $30 copay for outpatient, inpatient same as hospital
- Network: Enhanced provider network (8,000+ providers in-network)

## Employer Contribution

The employer contributes 75% of the employee-only premium for the Silver plan as the base contribution. This contribution is applied to whichever plan the employee selects. For example, the employer contributes $168.75 per month toward any plan's employee-only premium.

For dependent coverage, the employer contributes 50% of the additional cost for employee + spouse and employee + children tiers on the Silver plan.

## Health Savings Account (HSA) Compatibility

The Basic and Silver plans are HSA-eligible high-deductible health plans (HDHPs). Employees enrolled in these plans may contribute to a Health Savings Account with pre-tax dollars. The employer provides an annual HSA contribution of $500 (individual) or $1,000 (family) for employees enrolled in the Basic plan.

## Prescription Drug Formulary

All medical plans use a four-tier prescription drug formulary:
- Tier 1 (Generic): FDA-approved generic medications
- Tier 2 (Preferred Brand): Preferred brand-name medications
- Tier 3 (Non-Preferred Brand): Non-preferred brand-name medications
- Tier 4 (Specialty): Specialty medications for complex conditions

Prior authorization may be required for Tier 3 and Tier 4 medications. A 90-day mail-order option is available at 2.5x the 30-day copay amount.

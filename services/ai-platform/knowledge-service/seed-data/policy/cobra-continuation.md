# COBRA Continuation Coverage Policy

## Overview

The Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA) provides eligible employees and their dependents the right to continue group health coverage after a qualifying event that would otherwise result in loss of coverage. This policy applies to employers with 20 or more employees.

## Qualifying Events for Employees

- Voluntary or involuntary termination of employment (except for gross misconduct)
- Reduction in hours worked that results in loss of eligibility

Duration: Up to 18 months of continuation coverage.

## Qualifying Events for Dependents

- Death of the covered employee: up to 36 months
- Divorce or legal separation from the covered employee: up to 36 months
- Employee becomes eligible for Medicare: up to 36 months
- Dependent child ages out of eligibility: up to 36 months

## Disability Extension

If a qualified beneficiary is determined by the Social Security Administration to be disabled at any time during the first 60 days of COBRA coverage, the 18-month period may be extended to 29 months for all qualified beneficiaries.

## Second Qualifying Event

If a second qualifying event occurs during the initial 18-month COBRA period, coverage may be extended to a total of 36 months from the date of the initial qualifying event. Second qualifying events include divorce, legal separation, death of the former employee, or a dependent child's loss of dependent status.

## COBRA Premium

COBRA participants are responsible for the full cost of coverage (both employer and employee share) plus a 2% administrative fee. For the disability extension period (months 19-29), the premium may be up to 150% of the plan cost.

## COBRA Premium Payment Schedule

| Tier | Monthly Premium |
|------|----------------|
| Employee Only | $650 per month |
| Employee + Spouse | $1,300 per month |
| Employee + Child(ren) | $1,100 per month |
| Family | $1,750 per month |

Note: Premiums are subject to change at the beginning of each plan year.

## Election Timeline

1. Employer must notify the plan administrator within 30 days of the qualifying event.
2. Plan administrator must send COBRA election notice within 14 days.
3. Qualified beneficiary has 60 days from the later of the notice date or the date coverage would be lost to elect COBRA.
4. If elected, COBRA coverage is retroactive to the date of the qualifying event.

## Payment Timeline

- Initial payment is due within 45 days of electing COBRA.
- Subsequent payments are due on the first of each month.
- A 30-day grace period applies to monthly payments.
- Failure to pay within the grace period results in termination of COBRA coverage with no reinstatement.

## Coverage Under COBRA

COBRA coverage is identical to the active employee coverage at the time of the qualifying event. If the employer changes plan offerings for active employees, COBRA participants receive the same changes. COBRA participants may change coverage during open enrollment on the same terms as active employees.

## Termination of COBRA

COBRA coverage terminates on the earliest of:
- The end of the maximum coverage period (18, 29, or 36 months)
- Failure to pay premiums within the grace period
- The beneficiary becomes covered under another group health plan
- The beneficiary becomes entitled to Medicare
- The employer ceases to maintain any group health plan

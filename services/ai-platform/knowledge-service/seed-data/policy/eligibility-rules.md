# Employee Benefits Eligibility Rules

## General Eligibility

All full-time employees working 30 or more hours per week are eligible for benefits enrollment. Part-time employees working between 20 and 29 hours per week are eligible for limited benefits (medical and dental only). Temporary employees and independent contractors are not eligible for benefits.

## Eligibility Effective Date

New hire benefits become effective on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment. For example, an employee hired on January 15 becomes eligible on March 1. Employees hired on the first day of a month become eligible on the first day of the following month.

## Dependent Eligibility

Eligible dependents include:
- Legal spouse or registered domestic partner
- Biological, adopted, or step-children up to age 26
- Children up to age 26 who are full-time students
- Disabled dependents of any age who became disabled before age 26, with proof of disability

Dependents must be added within 30 days of a qualifying life event or during open enrollment. Proof of dependent relationship (marriage certificate, birth certificate, adoption decree) is required for all dependent additions.

## Waiting Period

There is a 30-day waiting period from the date of hire before benefits become active. During this waiting period, employees may submit their enrollment selections, which will become effective at the end of the waiting period.

## Enrollment Window

New employees must complete their benefits enrollment within 30 days of their eligibility date. Failure to enroll within this window will result in default enrollment in the basic medical plan only. All other benefits will be waived until the next open enrollment period.

## Termination of Eligibility

Benefits eligibility ends on the last day of the month in which employment terminates. Employees who are terminated, resign, or are laid off may be eligible for COBRA continuation coverage for up to 18 months (or 36 months for certain qualifying events).

## Rehire Eligibility

Employees who are rehired within 30 days of termination will have their previous benefit elections reinstated without a new waiting period. Employees rehired after 30 days are treated as new hires and must complete a new enrollment with the standard waiting period.

## Hours Reduction

If an employee's hours are reduced below the eligibility threshold (30 hours for full-time, 20 hours for part-time), they will maintain their current benefits through the end of the current month. They will then be offered COBRA continuation if their hours reduction makes them ineligible.

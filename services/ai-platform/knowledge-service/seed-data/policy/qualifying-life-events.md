# Qualifying Life Events Policy

## Overview

Under IRS Section 125, employees may make mid-year changes to their benefit elections only if they experience a qualifying life event (QLE). Changes must be consistent with the life event and must be requested within 30 days of the event.

## Qualifying Life Events

### Marriage or Domestic Partnership
Employees who marry or enter a registered domestic partnership may:
- Add their spouse/partner as a dependent
- Change plan tier to cover the new dependent
- Add the spouse/partner to dental, vision, and life insurance

Required documentation: Marriage certificate or domestic partnership registration.

### Divorce or Legal Separation
Employees who divorce or legally separate may:
- Remove the former spouse/partner from coverage
- Reduce plan tier if applicable
- The former spouse/partner is eligible for COBRA continuation

Required documentation: Divorce decree or legal separation agreement.

### Birth or Adoption of a Child
Employees who have a new child (birth, adoption, or placement for adoption) may:
- Add the child as a dependent
- Change plan tier to cover the new dependent
- Add the child to dental, vision, and life insurance
- Coverage for newborns is retroactive to the date of birth if enrolled within 30 days

Required documentation: Birth certificate, adoption decree, or placement agreement.

### Death of a Dependent
If a covered dependent passes away, the employee may:
- Remove the dependent from coverage
- Reduce plan tier if applicable
- Change other benefit elections as appropriate

Required documentation: Death certificate.

### Loss of Other Coverage
If an employee or dependent loses coverage under another plan (e.g., spouse's employer plan terminates), they may:
- Enroll in benefits if previously waived
- Add dependents who lost other coverage
- This includes loss of Medicaid, CHIP, or marketplace coverage

Required documentation: Letter of loss of coverage from the prior plan.

### Gain of Other Coverage
If an employee or dependent gains coverage under another plan, they may:
- Waive coverage
- Remove dependents who gained other coverage

Required documentation: Evidence of new coverage.

### Change in Employment Status
Changes that affect benefit eligibility include:
- Full-time to part-time transition (or vice versa)
- Leave of absence (FMLA, military, personal)
- Spouse's change in employment affecting their coverage

### Change in Residence
If an employee moves to a new service area where their current plan is not available, they may change to an available plan in their new area.

## Timing Requirements

All qualifying life event changes must be submitted within 30 calendar days of the event. The effective date of the change will be:
- Date of event for births and adoptions
- First of the month following the event for all other QLEs

Requests submitted more than 30 days after the qualifying event will be denied. The employee must then wait until the next open enrollment period.

## How to Submit a QLE Change

1. Submit the change through the benefits enrollment system within 30 days of the event.
2. Upload or provide required supporting documentation.
3. HR Benefits team reviews the request within 5 business days.
4. Approved changes are processed and confirmation is sent to the employee.
5. Denied requests may be appealed within 15 days of denial.

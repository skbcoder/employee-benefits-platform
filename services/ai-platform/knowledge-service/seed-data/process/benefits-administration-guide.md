# Benefits Administration Guide

## Overview

This guide outlines the procedures and responsibilities for administering the company's employee benefits program. It is intended for HR Benefits team members and plan administrators.

## Annual Benefits Cycle

### January - March: Plan Year Start
- Verify all open enrollment elections are active and accurate
- Process any late enrollment corrections from open enrollment
- Distribute updated Summary Plan Descriptions (SPDs) if plan changes occurred
- File Form 1094-C/1095-C with IRS (due January 31 for employee copies, March 31 electronic filing)
- Complete nondiscrimination testing for Section 125 cafeteria plan

### April - June: Mid-Year Administration
- Process qualifying life event changes within required timeframes
- Conduct quarterly audit of enrollment accuracy
- Review COBRA participant status and payment compliance
- Prepare mid-year benefits utilization report for management
- Address any ACA measurement period transitions for variable hour employees

### July - September: Renewal Preparation
- Receive renewal proposals from insurance carriers (typically 90 days before plan year)
- Analyze claims experience and utilization data
- Negotiate renewal rates with carriers
- Prepare cost comparison of renewal options
- File Form 5500 annual report (due July 31 for calendar year plans)
- Begin planning open enrollment communication strategy

### October - December: Open Enrollment
- Finalize plan designs and premium rates for upcoming year
- Distribute open enrollment materials (October 1)
- Conduct benefits fairs and information sessions (October 15)
- Open enrollment portal (November 1)
- Send reminder notices (November 15)
- Close enrollment (November 30)
- Process and verify all enrollment elections
- Send confirmation statements (December 15)
- Prepare for January 1 plan year start

## New Hire Processing

1. Employee is entered into HR system with hire date and employment classification
2. System calculates eligibility date (first of month following 30 days)
3. Benefits enrollment invitation is sent to employee's email on eligibility date
4. Employee has 30 days from eligibility date to complete enrollment
5. If enrollment is not completed within 30 days:
   - Employee is auto-enrolled in Basic medical plan
   - All other benefits are waived
   - Employee is notified of default enrollment
6. Enrollment is processed through the enrollment system
7. Carrier feeds are generated and sent to insurance carriers
8. Employee receives confirmation of active benefits

## Termination Processing

1. HR notifies Benefits team of termination date
2. Benefits team determines last day of coverage (last day of termination month)
3. COBRA qualifying event notice is generated
4. COBRA election notice is mailed within 14 days
5. If employee elects COBRA, coverage continues seamlessly
6. If employee does not elect COBRA within 60 days, coverage terminates retroactively
7. Final premium deductions are processed on the last paycheck
8. Any outstanding FSA claims must be submitted within 90 days

## Carrier Management

### Data Feeds
Enrollment data is transmitted to insurance carriers through electronic data interchange (EDI) feeds:
- Medical carrier: Daily eligibility feed (834 format)
- Dental carrier: Weekly eligibility feed
- Vision carrier: Weekly eligibility feed
- Life insurance carrier: Monthly eligibility feed

### Discrepancy Resolution
- Monthly reconciliation of enrollment records against carrier records
- Discrepancies must be resolved within 30 days
- Common discrepancy types: missing dependents, incorrect effective dates, retroactive terminations
- Carrier discrepancy contacts maintained in the vendor contact directory

### Service Level Agreements
| Carrier Service | SLA |
|----------------|-----|
| Claims processing | 15 business days |
| Prior authorization | 72 hours (urgent), 15 days (standard) |
| Member ID card issuance | 10 business days |
| Appeal determination | 30-60 days |
| Eligibility update | 48 hours from feed receipt |

## Privacy and Security

### Access Controls
- Benefits enrollment data access is restricted to authorized HR Benefits team members
- System access uses role-based permissions with unique credentials
- All access is logged and auditable
- PHI is encrypted in transit and at rest

### Record Retention
| Record Type | Retention Period |
|------------|-----------------|
| Enrollment records | 7 years after plan year end |
| COBRA records | 7 years after coverage ends |
| Form 5500 | 6 years |
| Form 1094-C/1095-C | 7 years |
| Claims records | 7 years |
| Plan documents | Permanent |
| HIPAA authorizations | 6 years |

### Breach Response
In the event of a suspected data breach involving PHI:
1. Immediately notify the Privacy Officer
2. Do not attempt to investigate independently
3. Preserve all evidence and access logs
4. Privacy Officer will initiate breach investigation per HIPAA Breach Notification procedures
5. Affected individuals will be notified within 60 days if breach is confirmed
